### 3. Light Management: Hacking Your Circadian Rhythm

Light is the most powerful signal for your internal body clock. 

- **Morning:** Get 10-15 minutes of direct sunlight within 30 minutes of waking. This anchors your circadian rhythm and boosts cortisol (in a good way) to promote alertness.
- **Evening:** Block out all blue light for at least 2 hours before bed. Blue light from screens and overhead lighting suppresses melatonin production.

**Your Toolkit:**

- **Blue Light Blocking Glasses:** A non-negotiable for evening screen time. Look for glasses that block 90-100% of blue light.
- **Blackout Curtains:** Make your bedroom a cave. Even small amounts of light can disrupt sleep.
- **Wake-Up Light:** A gentle way to wake up with light that mimics the sunrise, instead of a jarring alarm.

### 4. Supplements: The Final 5%

Supplements can be a powerful tool, but they should be the final piece of the puzzle after you've optimized your environment and behaviors.

- **Magnesium L-Threonate:** The most bioavailable form of magnesium for the brain, it promotes relaxation and deep sleep.
- **L-Theanine:** An amino acid found in green tea that reduces anxiety and improves sleep quality.
- **Apigenin:** A compound found in chamomile that has a mild sedative effect.

**Disclaimer:** Always consult with your doctor before starting any new supplement regimen.

## Your 30-Day Sleep Optimization Protocol

**Week 1: The Foundation**

- **Goal:** Establish a consistent sleep schedule (even on weekends).
- **Action:** Go to bed and wake up within the same 30-minute window every day.
- **Tech:** Start using a sleep tracker (Oura or WHOOP) to get baseline data.

**Week 2: Light & Darkness**

- **Goal:** Master your light exposure.
- **Action:** Morning sunlight, no screens 2 hours before bed (or use blue light blockers).
- **Tech:** Install blackout curtains in your bedroom.

**Week 3: Temperature Control**

- **Goal:** Create the perfect sleep climate.
- **Action:** Set your thermostat to 65-68째F.
